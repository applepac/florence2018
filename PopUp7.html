The night of July 11th, every car in Zadar was blaring, and happy guys on top of their car with Croatian/Slavic flags posed for every passerby. Rejoicing people invited us to take selfies. Police officers calmly and lovingly watched 20-something young men cheering (and sometimes smashing beer bottles). The city celebrated until 3. The next morning, Zadar went back to the clean, calm seaside town again.
An average Croatian young adult speaks four languages in near-perfection (English, German, Italian, Croatian). There seemed to be a dearly cherished yet long repressed national identity in people. They won (metaphorically), but people took the victory as their personal catharsis.
